SUN JUL 12 — SUN JUL 19 MILEAGE 16–18 HARD DAYS 2

2 weeks out from Nationals

It will be fun and exciting to work on things that simulate race week at Nationals! This is a chance to see how it feels to do hard days back-to-back and to dial in recovery. The point isn't to prove anything — it's to find out what it feels like, so nothing surprises you at Nationals.

Practice recovery this week - good sleep, good food, some mobility, cold plunges, etc. They consistently make a big difference in races.

Another focus will be mental strategies. This is a good week to:

  • Visualize the races. The energy at the start, the laps, how you'll feel. Come up with a plan to help you stay positive — and know it will be hard at times.
  • Dial in your mantra. Write it down on Tuesday. Use it in both workouts.
  • Practice having fun while doing hard things. Smile. Laugh sometimes.
  • Look back at the season. You have done so many awesome things with running this season. Hard training, fast races, new events — like a throws relay :). You have everything you need already.

Nationals is where you get to enjoy all of the hard work you put in this season.

The races will be fast and fun. Just like you.

16Thu

1500m Prelim Simulation

This workout will simulate the 1500m prelims 2 weeks from today. Keep it controlled and smooth.

  • It would be great to do this on a flat dirt road, bike path, or section of quiet road. You can use effort and feeling rather than actual paces. Also you can do it by time or distance.

    If you do it on a dirt road or if the place you find is not quite flat,definitely do it by time and effort NOT distance and actual paces.

  • Practice using your mantra during this workout.
  • Practice smiling at least once during each interval — it's been shown that brief periods of smiling actually improve running economy by 2–3%! Kipchoge (the 1:59 marathoner) does it deliberately at least once a mile during his races and workouts 😁️

Recovery Routine

This is what makes tomorrow possible. Try these things and use them to create a routine you can use at Nationals. Tap each one as you do it.

  • Eat within 30 minutes. Carbs first, some protein with them - chocolate milk, smoothies, milk shakes are all great options. Research shows that eating soon after you finish really helps with recovery.
  • Legs up the wall, 10–15 minutes.
  • Get in cold water. Maine has a lot of it.
  • Real meals the rest of the day.
  • Short nap if you can get one.
  • Legs up again a couple more times.
  • Long, good night of sleep.
